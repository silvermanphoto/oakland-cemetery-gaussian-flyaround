The sun assumption (corrected — not a 10 a.m. morning capture)

The brief assumed a ~10 a.m. December morning. The source frames say the flight ran 15:36:57 → 16:52:49 on Dec 22, 2022 — a late-afternoon capture (EXIF "Date taken 3:36 PM"; Atlanta EST; drone GPS confirms Atlanta). 15:36 UTC would be 10:36 EST, so the timezone was pinned two ways before trusting it.

NOAA solar position for that window: the sun tracked south-west, low and descending — midpoint azimuth ≈ 230°, elevation ≈ 12.7° (consistent with the ~5:33 p.m. sunset that day). With the georegistered frame (UE world +X = North, +Y = East, +Z = Up), the DirectionalLight is Pitch −12.73°, Yaw +50.11°, Roll 0 — a warm winter afternoon sun. One soft assumption remains: the drone's camera-clock accuracy (no GPS-time field stored to cross-check); direction is high-confidence, worth an eye-check against a baked shadow.
